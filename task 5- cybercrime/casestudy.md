# Task 5 – Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing

## What is Phishing?

Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers impersonate a trusted entity — such as a bank, government agency, or well-known company — to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, OTPs, credit card numbers, or login credentials.

## How It Typically Happens (Step-by-Step)

1. **The attacker selects a target** — often a student, employee, or online banking user.
2. **A deceptive message is crafted** — usually an email, SMS (smishing), or WhatsApp message that looks identical to a legitimate communication from a bank, college, or service provider (e.g., "Your VIT Bhopal account has been suspended. Click here to verify.").
3. **A fake link is included** — the link leads to a spoofed website that looks exactly like the real one (e.g., a fake SBI or Gmail login page).
4. **The victim enters their credentials** — believing the site is genuine, they type their username, password, and sometimes their OTP.
5. **The attacker collects the data** — the stolen credentials are captured instantly and used to access the victim's real account, steal money, or sell the data.

## Who is Typically Targeted?

- First-year college students unfamiliar with digital threats
- Online banking users
- Job seekers on platforms like Naukri.com or LinkedIn
- People who have recently made an online purchase

## Consequences

- Loss of money from bank accounts
- Identity theft and misuse of personal information
- Emotional distress and anxiety
- Legal complications if accounts are misused for fraud
- Difficulty recovering compromised accounts
