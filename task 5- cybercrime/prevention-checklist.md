# Stay Safe Online – Prevention Checklist
### For College Students in India

---

## General Cybersecurity Tips

1. **Never click on suspicious links** in emails, SMS, or WhatsApp messages — even if they appear to come from your bank, college, or a government agency. Always go directly to the official website by typing the URL.

2. **Check website URLs carefully** before entering any login or payment information. Look for "https://" and a padlock icon. Be alert for misspelled domain names (e.g., "sbi-bank-login.com" instead of "sbi.co.in").

3. **Use strong, unique passwords** for every account. A strong password is at least 8 characters long and includes a mix of uppercase letters, lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Use a password manager if needed.

4. **Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)** on all important accounts — email, social media, banking apps, and academic portals.

5. **Do not share personal information** such as your Aadhaar number, PAN card, date of birth, or college ID number with unverified sources online or over the phone.

6. **Keep your devices and apps updated.** Software updates often include security patches that protect against the latest threats.

---

## UPI and Financial Safety (Mandatory)

7. **Never share your UPI PIN, OTP, or bank password** with anyone — not even someone claiming to be from your bank's customer care. Legitimate banks never ask for these details.

8. **Be cautious of "money receive" requests on UPI apps.** Scammers often send fake payment requests and trick victims into entering their UPI PIN thinking they are receiving money — but they are actually authorising a payment out of their account.

---

## If You Are a Victim

- **Report cybercrime immediately** at the **National Cyber Crime Portal:** [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in)
- **Call the 24x7 Cyber Crime Helpline: 1930**
- Inform your bank immediately if financial fraud has occurred — they can often freeze or reverse transactions if reported promptly.
