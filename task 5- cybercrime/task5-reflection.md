## Cybercrime Type Chosen
**Phishing**

---

## Reflection

While researching phishing for this task, the most surprising thing I discovered was how convincing modern phishing websites actually look. Using freely available tools, attackers can build near-perfect replicas of official bank or government websites — complete with logos, colour schemes, and even fake security certificates. I had always assumed I would easily spot a fake website, but after looking at real documented examples, I am far less confident in that assumption.

What also surprised me was how widespread phishing is in India specifically. UPI-based phishing scams have grown rapidly, targeting college students and young users who transact digitally every day. The attackers do not need advanced technical skills — just a convincing fake message and a spoofed link.

The one habit I will personally change as a result of this research is to never click links directly from messages — even if they appear to come from a trusted source like my bank or college. Instead, I will always type the official URL directly into the browser or use a saved bookmark. This one habit alone can prevent the majority of phishing attacks.
